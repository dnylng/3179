World Happiness Report 2023

"Happiness depends upon ourselves" - Aristotle

The World Happiness Report is an annual publication that marks the ranks of happiness based on the well-being of their citizens. People are asked to evaluate their current lives on a scale of 0 to 10, with 10 representing the best possible life and 0 the worst possible life. It provides a holistic view of wellbeing.

Happiness Across the Globe

Around the globe happiness levels vary extremely, therforem it is essential to recognise the differences in happiness scores across both different regions and different sub-regions. To the right are boxplots that show the happiness levels for both categories.

Happiness Across Regions

Historically, European countries often rank high in happiness scores. Oceania also fares very well in terms of happiness, however there are not many countries in Oceania so the data is specific.

On the other hand, African countries often rank low in happiness scores. With some parts of Asia being ranked lowly as well. This can be due to a combination of many factors. It has consistenting been this way for the major regions across the previous years of the world happiness report.

Happiness Across Sub-Regions

Within larger regions, subregions can show significant variations in happiness. For instance East Asia might have a different distribution than those in South Asia or Southeach Asia.

Demonstrating that happiness for a region does not necessarily mean happiness for all subregions. This makes it almost ignorant to just look at data from a regional perspective and instead to have a more accurate representation of happiness, subregions should be considered as well.

Factors influencing Happiness

"Normal" Factors

The report examines several factors believed to influence happiness. These represent the actual, observed data points without transformation

GDP per capita: Economic performance of a country

Social support Having someone to count on in difficult times

Healthy life expectancy: Expected years of good health

Freedom to make life choices: The level of freedom in daily life

Generosity Measures of giving within a society

Perceptions of Corruption Widespread corruption believed in the country

"Explained by" refers to the factors that instead of raw values, they explain the variation in happiness scores between countries. Compare Australia/Malaysia against the top/bottom 5 happiest countries

Final Remarks

Most important Factors

From analysing the data above, the most important factors of happiness scores of countries are GDP per capita and Social support. Higher wealth in countries also suggest positive correlations in other factors such as life expectancy and social support.

Although it can provide vital information for governments to help countries further develop their infrastructure, one must remember that correlation does not imply causation.

Geogrophy

The highest scorers are located in Europe and Oceania. This is because these countries are more developed with their respective governments providing more support for their citizens and their existing infrastructure. However, these are not to be taken literally as happiness is still subjective but it is a good indicator

Language

The most spoken languages in the world are Mandarin, Spanish, and English. However, the countries that speak these languages do not necessarily have the highest happiness scores. This is because happiness is not only influenced by language, but also by geography, culture, and other factors. Although English is one of the most spoken languages, this does not have that much of a global correlation. We see that European countires that speak German and French have higher happiness scores.
